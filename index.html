Original Research Health Equity Survey Study
The Right to Thrive: Championing Healthcare Accessibility for a Stronger Generation
Florida Atlantic University · Independent survey research
An original 17-question survey of 198 adults analyzing barriers to healthcare access. Findings showed transportation, affordability, and lack of insurance as the most prevalent barriers — with statistically significant differences by income and gender (χ² analysis, p<.01).
Vedaant Mutha · Sole author

About

Hi, I'm Vedaant.

I'm a pre-medical student at the University of Florida studying Economics and Biology — a combination that shapes how I think about medicine: not just as science, but as a system people have to navigate to get well.

My research spans immunology and gene therapy, with peer-reviewed publications on oxidative stress in autoimmune disease and gene-therapy approaches to Type 1 diabetes. Alongside the lab work, I run original studies on healthcare access and lead outreach that brings screenings and care directly to people experiencing homelessness.

Whether I'm analyzing biosimilar economics, screening blood pressure at Grace Marketplace, or co-authoring a systematic review, the throughline is the same: everyone deserves the right to thrive.
